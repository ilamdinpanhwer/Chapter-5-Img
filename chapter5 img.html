Public Holidays ln Pakistan

Kashmir Day

Kashmir Day in Pakistan has been a national holiday since 1990, when Punjab state leader Nawaz
Sharif called for a national strike to show support for the struggle for freedom of the people of
Kashmir.

Pakistan Day

Pakistan Day is a commemoration of Pakistan’s declaration of independence from British rule and the
adoption of a national constitution in 1956.

Labour Day

Labour Day is an official holiday observed on the first day of May in Pakistan and in around 80 other
countries, aside from many others where it is unofficially observed.

Eid ul-Fitr 2023

Eid ul-Fitr is celebrated in Pakistan with much religious fervour and general merriment. The national
holiday comes on the first day of the Islamic month
of Shawwal, immediately following the holy month of Ramadan and month-long period of fasting, prayer and devotion. It is one of the most important of all Muslim commemorations.

Eid ul-Azha 2023

Eid ul Azha is the most significant holiday on the Islamic calendar, both in Pakistan
and throughout the Muslim world.

Ashura

Ashura is a public holiday in Pakistan, as it is in many other nations with large Muslim populations,
including neighbouring Afghanistan and India.

Independence Day

Pakistan was granted independence on 14 August and India on 15 August – the difference of a
day being planned so that the British viceroy could attend both transfer of power ceremonies.

Milad un-Nabi

n Pakistan, the majority Sunni Muslim population celebrates Prophet Muhammad’s Birthday on 12
Rabi al-Awwal, while the minority Shias keep up festivities on 17 Rabi. The official public holiday follows br the Sunni calculation.

Quaid-e-Azam Day

On December 25 each year, Pakistan celebrates the birth anniversary of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah.
He was an Indian politician who campaigned for an independent Pakistan, becoming its first
leader. Quaid-e-Azam translates to “Great Leader” in Pakistan
